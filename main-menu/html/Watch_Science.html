Watch Science

Timekeeping is not just an art — it's a science of precision, mechanics, and innovation. At O'clock, we are fascinated by the inner workings of watches, and we’re here to share that fascination with you.

Inside Every Watch

Whether quartz or mechanical, a watch is a marvel of engineering. Tiny gears, springs, rotors, and jewels work in harmony to measure the passing seconds with breathtaking accuracy.

  • Quartz Movement: Powered by a battery and a vibrating crystal for high precision.
  • Mechanical Movement: Driven by a mainspring and regulated by a balance wheel — no battery required.
  • Automatic Watches: Self-winding through wrist motion, merging art and physics.

The Science of Accuracy

Time is measured in hertz (Hz) in watches. A typical quartz watch vibrates at 32,768 Hz, while mechanical watches tick at 2.5 to 4 Hz (18,000 to 28,800 vibrations per hour). The higher the frequency, the smoother the second hand sweep and better the accuracy.

Materials & Innovation

  • Sapphire Crystal: Ultra-hard and scratch-resistant, used for watch faces.
  • Titanium & Ceramic: Durable, lightweight, and hypoallergenic materials.
  • Super-LumiNova: Glows in the dark, aiding visibility at night.
  • Smartwatch Sensors: Heart rate, motion tracking, solar charging, and more.

Why It Matters

A watch is not just an accessory — it’s a fusion of physics, engineering, and tradition. Understanding the science behind it deepens your appreciation for every tick and tock.
